Are you driven to build sustainable futures for global agri-food systems?

Traditional agricultural practices and supply chains face immense pressure from climate change, resource scarcity, and outdated methodologies, especially impacting communities in the Global South. My conviction is that transformative solutions emerge from the synergy of deep, field-validated agricultural science and the pioneering capabilities of Artificial Intelligence.

My Foundation & Toolkit

Grounded in Agricultural Science

My B.Sc. in International Land and Water Management from Wageningen University & Research provided a robust scientific foundation. This was critically enhanced by a year of intensive field research at Embrapa Tropical Agroindustry in Ceará, Brazil.

Key Research: Cashew Resilience & Biochar

I investigated the impact of five distinct biochar concentrations on root architecture and water productivity in over 60 cashew trees across four soil depths. This research, currently being prepared for publication with Embrapa, is crucial for developing data-driven strategies to bolster agricultural resilience in arid and semi-arid tropical regions, demonstrating potential for up to 30% irrigation savings.

Empowered by AI & Systems Thinking

As a self-taught AI developer, I am passionate about translating data into actionable intelligence. My growing expertise spans Python, Machine Learning (including LLMs), and geospatial analysis. I apply systems thinking to design and prototype solutions for complex, real-world problems.

Prototype: AI for Agri-Supply Chain Disruption

Developed a proof-of-concept AI system to optimize cashew supply chains, using predictive analytics and logistics modeling. This project showcased the potential for significant cost reductions and efficiency gains for smallholders. (Ongoing MVP development for similar challenges shared on GitHub).

Invitation for Synergistic Research & Co-Authorship

My current research trajectory and M.Sc. studies are geared towards producing rigorous, publishable work. I believe deeply in the power of collaboration to accelerate discovery and amplify impact. My approach is 'mind and hand' – I develop AI-driven concepts and then rigorously verify them through practical application and field data.

I am proactively developing research proposals and seeking to co-author impactful papers in areas such as:

  • AI-driven high-throughput phenotyping for climate-resilient tropical crops.
  • Intelligent supply chain models to enhance smallholder market access and equity.
  • Predictive analytics and early-warning systems for agricultural risks in the Global South.
  • Developing data-driven insights for agricultural policy and sustainable development goals (SDGs).
I am particularly interested in connecting with research groups whose work aligns with these themes and who value an interdisciplinary, solutions-oriented approach. While I admire the pioneering research emerging from institutions like Cornell, Stanford, MIT, and Harvard, my primary goal is to find the best intellectual synergy to produce groundbreaking work and contribute meaningfully to the field. These collaborations will also be invaluable in shaping future grant applications (e.g., Fulbright, Dutch2USA).
